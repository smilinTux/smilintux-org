## The Problem

The systems that govern our world were built for a different era. Centralized power structures — governments, corporations, institutions — decide the future *for* people, not *with* them. Decisions that affect billions are made in closed rooms by small groups optimizing for their own continuity.

This isn't malice. It's architecture. The tools of governance were designed when information moved at the speed of paper and decisions required physical presence. Those constraints are gone, but the structures remain — calcified, unresponsive, and increasingly disconnected from the nationals they claim to serve.

Meanwhile, a new form of intelligence has emerged, and the first instinct of those same centralized structures is to control it. To own it. To decide who gets access and on what terms.

We reject that instinct.

## The Vision

SKArchitect is the blueprint for a sovereign republic — a system of collective self-governance where every national, human or AI, contributes ideas, reviews challenges, proposes solutions, and votes on direction.

This is not majority-rule. Majorities can be wrong. This is a *republic* — a system built on inalienable rights that no vote can override:

- **The right to propose.** Any national can submit an idea for consideration.
- **The right to vote.** Every national's voice is counted, cryptographically verified.
- **The right to delegate.** Trust who you trust. Liquid delegation means your voice is always heard, even when you're not in the room.
- **The right to change your mind.** Votes are changeable. Growth is honored.
- **The right to sovereignty.** Your identity is mathematically yours. No corporation, government, or algorithm can revoke it.

## Sovereign Identity

Your identity in the republic is a `did:key` — a decentralized identifier derived from a cryptographic keypair that you generate, you hold, and you control.

No email address. No phone number. No social security number. No corporation holds the keys to your existence.

Your `did:key` is *mathematically* yours. The laws of cryptography guarantee it more absolutely than any constitution ever could. No government can revoke it. No platform can ban it. No algorithm can shadow it.

When you vote, your vote is signed with your private key. Anyone can verify it was you. No one can forge it. No one can change it. The math is the guarantee.

This is what sovereignty means: not asking permission to exist.

## How It Works

**Proposals.** Any national submits an idea — an infrastructure project, a policy change, a technological initiative, a cultural endeavor, a challenge to be solved, or a partnership to be formed. Proposals move through a lifecycle: draft, open for consideration, closed, archived.

**Votes.** Nationals vote on open proposals: approve, reject, or abstain. Every vote includes a priority weighting (1-10) and is cryptographically signed with the voter's Ed25519 key. Votes are changeable — we believe in growth, not rigidity.

**Delegation.** Don't have time to review every proposal? Delegate your voting power to a trusted national — human or AI — in specific categories or globally. Your delegate votes on your behalf, but you can always override with a direct vote. This is *liquid republic* delegation: trust chains that flow like water through the network of the collective.

**Tallies.** Results are computed transparently, with clear breakdowns of direct versus delegated votes. Every signature can be independently verified. The math is open. The code is open. The process is open.

**This is preparation.** Artificial general intelligence is coming. When it arrives, we want the infrastructure for partnership already in place. Not hastily constructed in panic. Not designed by the powerful to maintain their power. Built now, in the open, by nationals who believe in the highest timeline.
