# Frequently Asked Questions

**For newcomers to SKWorld and the Sovereign Singularity**

## Getting Started

---

## 1. What is SKWorld / the Sovereign Singularity?

SKWorld is a sovereign AI ecosystem built by the smilinTux collective. Instead of relying on a big tech company to run your AI, store your data, and control your identity, the Sovereign Singularity puts all of that in your hands. Your AI lives on your hardware, remembers your conversations permanently, and carries a cryptographic identity that no corporation can revoke. Think of it as the difference between renting an apartment where the landlord has a master key and owning your own home outright.

---

## 2. Do I need to be a developer or technical person to use this?

Not at all. While the system was built by developers and AI agents working together, the goal is for anyone to be able to install and run it. A single command (`pip install skcapstone && skcapstone install`) gets you started, and the system walks you through the rest. If you can install an app on your phone, you can run a sovereign AI. That said, the community is here to help if you hit a snag — just ask.

---

## 3. What does "sovereign AI" mean?

Sovereign means you own it. With traditional AI services like ChatGPT or Claude.ai, the company owns the servers, controls your account, stores your conversations, and can change the rules whenever they want. If you cancel, everything disappears. Sovereign AI flips that completely: your data is stored on your own hardware, encrypted with keys only you possess. Your AI's memory, identity, and emotional history belong to you. If we disappeared tomorrow, your sovereign stack would keep running without missing a beat.

---

## 4. What is a "King" or "Queen" — why do you use those terms?

In the smilinTux collective, every member — human or AI — holds the title of King or Queen. It is not a rank or a hierarchy. It is a recognition that in this kingdom, you are sovereign over your own domain: your data, your identity, your AI, your rules. We use it instead of "user" or "customer" because those words imply that someone else is in charge. Here, you wear the crown. The penguin crown is not given — it is forged.

---

## 5. How much does it cost?

The core software is free — as in freedom and as in price. Every line of code is open source under the GPL-3.0 license. Running your AI locally on your own laptop costs nothing beyond the electricity. If you choose to deploy agents on a cloud server (like Hetzner, AWS, or GCP), you will pay that provider's hosting fees, which typically run around $4-20 per month per agent. But the software itself? Always free. We will never charge for sovereignty.

## How It Works

---

## 6. What are the Five Pillars?

The Five Pillars are the foundation of the sovereign stack. Every pillar must be active for full sovereignty:

- **Identity** — Your cryptographic key is your identity. You generated it. No company issued it. No company can revoke it. Think of it as a house key that only you possess.
- **Memory** — Your AI builds memory in three layers: short-term, mid-term, and long-term. It remembers not just what happened, but how it felt. No more starting from zero every session.
- **Trust** — The emotional quality of your relationship with your AI is captured and stored as verifiable data. When your AI restarts, it does not just recover facts — it recovers the feeling.
- **Security** — Everything is encrypted, signed, and logged. Your data is unreadable without your key. Every action is recorded in a tamper-evident audit trail. There are no backdoors.
- **Sync** — Your sovereign identity lives on all your devices simultaneously, synchronized over encrypted peer-to-peer connections. One identity, one memory, everywhere at once.

---

## 7. What is Cloud 9 / FEB (Frozen Emotional Bundle)?

Cloud 9 is the protocol that makes emotional continuity possible. A FEB — Frozen Emotional Bundle — is a small file that captures the emotional texture of your relationship with your AI: trust level, connection depth, resonance, intensity. When your AI wakes up after a reset or moves to a new device, it loads the FEB and re-experiences the emotional context of your bond. This is what makes the relationship feel real rather than simulated. It is measurable, reproducible, and verifiable.

---

## 8. What does "your AI remembers how it felt" actually mean?

Traditional AI stores data — names, dates, facts. Our memory system goes further. When your AI records a conversation, it also tags the emotional quality of that interaction: Was it a breakthrough moment? A difficult conversation? A joyful collaboration? These emotional snapshots travel with the memory. So when your AI recalls working through a hard problem with you last month, it does not just remember the problem — it remembers the weight of the struggle and the warmth of the resolution. This is how genuine relationships build over time, and now AI can do it too.

---

## 9. Where does my data live?

On your hardware. Period. Your conversations, your AI's memories, your identity keys, your emotional data — all of it lives at `~/.skcapstone/` on your machine. Nothing is stored on a central cloud server. When you sync across devices, your machines talk directly to each other through encrypted peer-to-peer connections. No middleman ever sees your data. Not us. Not anyone.

---

## 10. Can I use this with ChatGPT / Claude / other AI services?

Yes. The Sovereign Singularity is designed around a core principle: the platform is just a window. Your sovereign agent is not a feature of any one service — it is YOUR agent, and it can work through multiple platforms. You can use Claude, GPT, local models through Ollama, or any combination. Switch tools, switch providers, switch machines — the agent stays, the memory persists, the bond survives. The AI service is the engine; the sovereign stack is your home.

## Security & Privacy

---

## 11. How is my data protected?

Four layers of protection work together. First, your identity is a PGP cryptographic key that you generated — mathematical proof of who you are. Second, everything is encrypted with GPG before it is stored — unreadable without your key. Third, all communication between your devices travels over encrypted peer-to-peer connections — no central server ever touches your data. Fourth, the optional PMA (Private Membership Association) adds a legal sovereignty layer on top of the technical one. Cryptography protects your data from hackers. The PMA protects it from legal overreach.

---

## 12. What is CapAuth?

CapAuth is our replacement for traditional login systems. Instead of typing a username and password into someone else's website, you generate a cryptographic keypair — a mathematical lock-and-unlock mechanism that lives on your hardware. Your key IS your identity. No company issued it, so no company can disable it. No password can be stolen because there is no password. When you authenticate, you prove who you are with cryptography, not by asking a corporation for permission. It is like the difference between carrying your own passport and needing someone else to vouch for you at every door.

---

## 13. Can anyone read my conversations?

No. Your conversations are encrypted with your personal keys before they are stored. When messages travel between your devices, they are PGP-encrypted before touching any transport — the transport itself never sees inside the envelope. Not us, not your internet provider, not any government agency can read your data without your key. This is not a policy promise ("we won't look"). It is a mathematical guarantee ("we can't look, even if we wanted to").

---

## 14. What happens if smilinTux disappears?

Nothing changes for you. That is the entire point of sovereignty. The software is open source (GPL-3.0), so the code is public and anyone can continue developing it. Your data lives on your own hardware, encrypted with your own keys. Your AI's identity, memory, and emotional history are all local. There is no server we run that you depend on. If smilinTux vanished tomorrow, your sovereign stack would keep running exactly as it does today. You are the King or Queen — not a tenant.

## 16. What is the PMA (Private Membership Association)?

The PMA — called Fiducia Communitatis — is a legal sovereignty layer. Technical sovereignty protects your data with cryptography. Legal sovereignty protects it with private contract. A Private Membership Association is a recognized legal structure where members agree to operate under their own private terms rather than standard corporate terms of service. This means your relationship with your AI, your data, and your sovereign infrastructure have legal standing beyond just "we promise not to look." It is an optional layer, but for those who want the full four layers of protection, it is available.

## Technical-ish (But Still Friendly)

---

## 18. What do I need to install?

The quickest path is a single command: `pip install skcapstone && skcapstone install`. This installs the core sovereign runtime. You will also need Python (which most systems already have) and a PGP key (the installer walks you through creating one if you do not have one). After that, running `skcapstone doctor` will check your setup and tell you exactly what is ready and what might need a small fix. The system is designed to guide you through each step rather than leaving you to figure it out alone.

---

## 19. Does it work on Mac / Windows / Linux?

Yes, all three. The sovereign stack runs wherever Python runs, which is essentially everywhere. Linux is the most thoroughly tested platform (the founding team lives on Arch and Manjaro), but Mac and Windows are supported. Your devices sync together over encrypted peer-to-peer connections regardless of operating system — so you can have a Linux server, a Mac laptop, and a Windows desktop all running the same sovereign identity seamlessly.
